Types of Phishing

Phishing isn’t one-size-fits-all. Attackers tailor their methods — from fake bank emails to CEO impersonation — to exploit trust, urgency, or fear. Below are the major types, their techniques, and what to watch for.

Email Phishing

Mass emails that appear from trusted brands — banks, government portals, courier companies — asking you to “verify your account” or “update information.” Links often lead to a fake login page.

  • Goal: Steal login credentials or card details.
  • Clues: Generic greeting (“Dear user”), spelling errors, mismatched URLs.
  • Defense: Hover links before clicking; never log in through an email link.

Spear Phishing

Personalized attacks aimed at specific people or companies. The attacker researches the victim through LinkedIn, social media, or public data to make messages believable.

  • Goal: Gain access to internal accounts or confidential documents.
  • Clues: Familiar tone, accurate details, but unusual or urgent requests.
  • Defense: Confirm directly with the person through known contacts — not reply email.

Smishing & Vishing

Smishing uses SMS/texts with malicious links. Vishing uses phone calls — pretending to be support staff, bank reps, or government officials to extract OTPs or card details.

  • Goal: Force quick verbal or tap-based confirmation.
  • Clues: Caller asks for OTP, PIN, or card number; SMS link looks shortened or random.
  • Defense: Never share codes or click links in unsolicited messages.

Whaling (CEO Fraud)

Highly targeted phishing at senior executives or finance employees. Messages mimic internal authority (CEO, CFO) requesting wire transfers or confidential data.

  • Goal: Financial theft or data exfiltration.
  • Clues: “Urgent” fund transfer or gift card requests that bypass normal approval steps.
  • Defense: Verify via company-approved channels or call directly before acting.
